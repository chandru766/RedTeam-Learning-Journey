## 🧠 What I Learned

### 🌐 Network
- A network is a group of connected devices that communicate with each other  
- Enables data sharing and communication  

---

### 🕸️ Types of Networks
- **LAN (Local Area Network)** – Small area (home, office)  
- **WAN (Wide Area Network)** – Large area (internet)  
- **MAN (Metropolitan Area Network)** – City-level network  

---

### 🖧 Network Devices
- **Router** – Connects different networks  
- **Switch** – Connects devices within a network  
- **Hub** – Broadcasts data to all devices (less secure)  

---

### 🔄 ARP (Address Resolution Protocol)
- Converts IP address into MAC address  
- Helps devices communicate within a local network  

---

### 💻 NIC (Network Interface Card)
- Hardware that connects a device to a network  
- Each NIC has a unique MAC address  

---

### 📡 Broadcast Domain
- A network area where broadcast messages reach all devices  
- Routers separate broadcast domains  

---

### 💥 Collision Domain
- A network area where data packets can collide  
- Switches reduce collision domains  

---

### 🌍 IP Address
- Unique identifier assigned to a device in a network  
- Example: 192.168.1.1  

---

### 📦 IP Allocation
- **Static IP** – Manually assigned  
- **Dynamic IP** – Automatically assigned (DHCP)  

---

## 🛠️ Practical Understanding

- Understood how devices communicate in a network  
- Learned how ARP maps IP to MAC addresses  
- Identified differences between network devices  
- Understood how IP addresses are assigned  

---

## 💡 Key Takeaways

- Networking is the backbone of cybersecurity  
- ARP plays a key role in local communication  
- Routers and switches manage network traffic efficiently  
- IP addressing is essential for device identification
