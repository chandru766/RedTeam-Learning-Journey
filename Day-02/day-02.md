## 🧠 What I Learned

### 🌐 Cyberspace
- The digital environment where communication and data exchange happen  
- Includes networks, systems, and the internet  

---

### ⚠️ Vulnerability
- A weakness in a system that can be exploited by attackers  

---

### 💣 Exploit
- A method or code used to take advantage of a vulnerability  

---

### 📦 Payload
- The actual malicious action executed after exploitation  
- Example: opening a backdoor or stealing data  

---

## 🦠 Malware (Malicious Software)

Software designed to harm or exploit systems.

---

### 🔐 Ransomware
- Locks or encrypts data and demands payment for access  

---

### 🐴 Trojans
- Malicious software disguised as legitimate programs  

---

### 🕵️ Spyware
- Secretly monitors user activity and steals information  

---

### 🦠 Viruses
- Infect files and spread when executed  

---

### 🐛 Worms
- Self-replicating malware that spreads across networks  

---

### 📢 Adware
- Displays unwanted advertisements  

---

### 🤖 Botnets
- A network of infected systems controlled by an attacker  

---

### ⌨️ Keyloggers
- Records keystrokes to capture sensitive data like passwords  

---

### 🕳️ Rootkits
- Hides malicious activity and provides unauthorized access  

---

### ⛏️ Cryptojacking
- Uses victim’s system to mine cryptocurrency without permission  

---

## 🛠️ Practical Understanding

- Understood how vulnerabilities lead to exploitation  
- Learned how different types of malware operate  
- Identified how attackers use payloads after gaining access  

---

## 💡 Key Takeaways

- Vulnerabilities are the starting point of attacks  
- Exploits and payloads are used to compromise systems  
- Malware comes in many forms with different purposes
